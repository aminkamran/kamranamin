About Me

Kamran Amin is currently working as a Special Research Assistant (A class) at the National Center for Nanoscience and Technology, CAS. He graduated with a BS degree from Preston University and MS and PhD degrees from the University of Chinese Academy of Sciences. He was a CAS TWAS Fellow for PhD at the National Center for Nanoscience and Technology (NCNST). During his studies, he was awarded the Excellent International Student (2018) and Excellent International Graduate (2021) by UCAS. He also won the NCNST Director Award for Excellent Research in 2017, 2018, 2019, and 2020.

His research mainly focuses on the design and synthesis of novel organic materials and the fabrication of flexible devices for energy storage applications. He has published several first-author papers in prestigious journals like Advanced Materials and Nano Energy. Besides, he served as the representative of international students at UCAS for the past 2 years.

Dr. Amin has recently partnered with the multinational company WAVETEC to establish a lithium-ion cell manufacturing facility in Pakistan. Through this collaboration, they have achieved a significant milestone by successfully creating the first commercial lithium-ion cell in the country. This breakthrough not only marks a technological achievement but also opens up numerous entrepreneurship and business opportunities in the growing field of lithium-ion cell production in Pakistan.
